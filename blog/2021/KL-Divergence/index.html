KL Divergence between two normal distributions

March 10, 2021

Here I would like to drive the KL divergence between two normal distribution, which is used in variational auto-encoders.

KL divergence

The KL divergence between two distributions \(p\) and \(q\) is defined as

\[D_{KL}(p||q) := \int_x p(x) \log \frac{p(x)}{q(x)} = \mathbf{E}_p[\log p(x) - \log q(x)]\]

KL divergence between two Gaussian

Now consider \(p(\mathbf{x}) \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu_p,\Sigma_p)\) and \(q(x) \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu_q,\Sigma_q)\) are probability density function of multivariate normal distributions where e.g.,

\[p(\mathbf{x}) = \frac{1}{(2\pi)^{k/2}|\Sigma_p|^{1/2}} \exp\left( -\frac{1}{2} (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p)^\top \Sigma_p^{-1} (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p) \right)\]

The KL divergence becomes

\[D_{KL}(p||q) = \mathbf{E}_p[\log p(x) - \log q(x)]\] \[D_{KL}(p||q) = \mathbf{E}_p\left[\frac{1}{2}\log \frac{|\Sigma_q|}{|\Sigma_p|} -\frac{1}{2} (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p)^\top \Sigma_p^{-1} (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p) + \frac{1}{2} (\mathbf{x}-\mu_q)^\top \Sigma_q^{-1} (\mathbf{x}-\mu_q) \right]\] \[D_{KL}(p||q) = \mathbf{E}_p\left[\frac{1}{2}\log \frac{|\Sigma_q|}{|\Sigma_p|}\right] - \frac{1}{2} \mathbf{E}_p\left[ (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p)^\top \Sigma_p^{-1} (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p)\right] + \frac{1}{2} \mathbf{E}_p\left[ (\mathbf{x}-\mu_q)^\top \Sigma_q^{-1} (\mathbf{x}-\mu_q) \right]\] \[D_{KL}(p||q) = \frac{1}{2}\log \frac{|\Sigma_q|}{|\Sigma_p|} - \frac{1}{2} \mathbf{E}_p\left[ (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p)^\top \Sigma_p^{-1} (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p)\right] + \frac{1}{2} \mathbf{E}_p\left[ (\mathbf{x}-\mu_q)^\top \Sigma_q^{-1} (\mathbf{x}-\mu_q) \right]\]

Focusing on the second term we obtain

\[\mathbf{E}_p\left[ (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p)^\top \Sigma_p^{-1} (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p)\right] = \mathbf{E}_p\left[ \text{tr} \left\{ (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p)^\top \Sigma_p^{-1} (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p)\right\}\right] = \mathbf{E}_p\left[ \text{tr} \left\{ (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p)^\top (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p)\Sigma_p^{-1} \right\}\right] = \text{tr}\left\{ \mathbf{E}_p\left[ (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p)^\top (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p)\Sigma_p^{-1} \right] \right\} = \text{tr}\left\{ \mathbf{E}_p\left[ (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p)^\top (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p)\right] \Sigma_p^{-1} \right\} = \text{tr}\left\{ \Sigma_p \Sigma_p^{-1} \right\} = \text{tr} (I_k) = k\]

The third term also becomes

\[\mathbf{E}_p\left[ (\mathbf{x}-\mu_q)^\top \Sigma_q^{-1} (\mathbf{x}-\mu_q)\right] = \mathbf{E}_p\left[ \text{tr} \left\{ (\mathbf{x}-\mu_q)^\top \Sigma_q^{-1} (\mathbf{x}-\mu_q)\right\}\right] = \mathbf{E}_p\left[ \text{tr} \left\{ (\mathbf{x}-\mu_q)^\top (\mathbf{x}-\mu_q)\Sigma_q^{-1} \right\}\right] = \text{tr}\left\{ \mathbf{E}_p\left[ (\mathbf{x}-\mu_q)^\top (\mathbf{x}-\mu_q)\Sigma_q^{-1} \right] \right\} = \text{tr}\left\{ \mathbf{E}_p\left[ (\mathbf{x}-\mu_q)^\top (\mathbf{x}-\mu_q)\right] \Sigma_q^{-1} \right\} = \text{tr}\left\{ \mathbf{E}_p\left[ (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p + \mu_p -\mu_q)^\top (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p + \mu_p - \mu_q)\right] \Sigma_q^{-1} \right\} = \text{tr}\left\{ \mathbf{E}_p\left[ (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p )^\top (\mathbf{x}-\mu_p )\right] \Sigma_q^{-1} + (\mu_p - \mu_q)^\top (\mu_p - \mu_q) \Sigma_q^{-1} \right\} = \text{tr}\left( \Sigma_p \Sigma_q^{-1}\right) + (\mu_p - \mu_q)^\top \Sigma_q^{-1}(\mu_p - \mu_q)\]

Replacing back into the first equation, we find that

\[D_{KL}(p||q) = \frac{1}{2} \left( \log \frac{|\Sigma_q|}{|\Sigma_p|}- k + \text{tr}\left( \Sigma_p^{-1} \Sigma_q^{-1}\right) + (\mu_p - \mu_q)^\top \Sigma_q^{-1}(\mu_p - \mu_q) \right)\]

Now, if $q\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I)$, we find that

\[D_{KL}(p||q) = \frac{1}{2} \left( -\log {|\Sigma_p|}- k + \text{tr}\left( \Sigma_p \right) + \mu_p^\top\mu_p \right)\]
