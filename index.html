I am currently a research scientist in Meta working on AI/ML for AR/VR applications.

Prior to that, I was a postdoctoral fellow in Applied Mathematics in School of Engineering and Applied Sciences at Harvard University working jointly with Professors Katia Bertoldi and Ariel Amir.

I received my Ph.D./M.Sc. in Engineering at UC Berkeley. I was advised by Professor M. Reza Alam. I got my second M.A. in Applied Mathematics at Berkeley advised by Prof. Jon Wilkening. In my thesis, I found a transformation to map hydrodynamics Euler’s equations to the Schr"odinger equation (link). Prior to my graduate studies, I got my B.Sc. in Engineering and B.Sc. in Physics from Sharif University of Technology.

You can find my mathematical genealogy in here. It was interesting to see Newton, Gauss, Laplace, Fourier, Euler, Hilbert, Bernoulli, Leibniz, Dirichelet, etc. in my genealogy tree. It was even more interesting to find out that the tree begins with Nasir al-Din Tusi, well-known persian polymath in 13th century.
